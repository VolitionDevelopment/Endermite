About Us

You know what you never see anymore? A server where you spawn in, walk outside, and build.

We at Endermite are going back to the days of Early Beta when combat wasn't the norm and there were no minigames. We're making this server around how Minecraft was meant to be played.

Think back, if you're old enough, to the glory days. Servers were simple places where people could build entire communities out of nothing. Minecraft didn't need minigames - if you wanted to shoot people you'd fire up GTA.

That is our vision. We want you to have complete freedom while still having peace of mind. That is why griefing is not allowed and PVP is disabled.

Why We're Doing It

This server is for people who are tired of PVP and minigames. This is for people who enjoy the survival and building aspect of Single Player but want to play with friends. This server is for people who enjoy Realms but wish for more people to play with and more functionality.

This server is for people who LOVED the early days - because even though we run the latest version of Minecraft, it will feel like Beta 1.8.1

The Concept

Towns

The first central theme for the server is player-run towns. The Capital of the town will be built by staff, but the leadership will be assigned to an ordinary player who we trust to run things. From there, players will be free to expand the town as they please.

Community

The second central theme of the server is community. While players are free to play by themselves, they will enjoy benefits if they join a town. On occasion, town vaults will be stocked with building materials free for citizens to use. Mayors will be regular players that are respected by the community.

Freedom

The final central theme of our server is freedom. As the towns will be run by regular players, they can decide the direction of the town. They can have a medieval town, full of castles and stone. Or perhaps a more modern town, full of glass and white clay. Once a town is created by the staff and a Mayor is chosen, it will be up to the players to decide the path they will go down.
